# Stakeholder Discussion Guide
## AI-Powered Multi-Tenant School Management Platform

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** January 19, 2026  
**Purpose**: Guide for conducting stakeholder interviews and validation sessions

---

## Interview Objectives

### Primary Goals
1. **Validate Problem**: Confirm pain points and challenges
2. **Understand Workflows**: Learn current processes and tools
3. **Identify Needs**: Discover must-have vs. nice-to-have features
4. **Gather Insights**: Uncover hidden requirements and expectations
5. **Build Relationships**: Establish trust and collaboration

### Secondary Goals
- Identify potential pilot schools
- Understand budget constraints
- Learn about decision-making process
- Discover competitive landscape insights

---

## Stakeholder Segments

### 1. School Administrators (Principals/Heads)
**Target**: 10-15 interviews  
**Duration**: 45-60 minutes each

### 2. Teachers
**Target**: 15-20 interviews  
**Duration**: 30-45 minutes each

### 3. Students
**Target**: 10-15 interviews  
**Duration**: 20-30 minutes each

### 4. Parents
**Target**: 8-10 interviews  
**Duration**: 20-30 minutes each

### 5. IT/Operations Staff
**Target**: 3-5 interviews  
**Duration**: 30-45 minutes each

## Interview Questions by Persona

## School Administrator Interview Guide

### Introduction (5 minutes)
- Thank you for your time
- Purpose: Understanding school management challenges
- Confidentiality assurance
- Permission to record (if applicable)

### Background & Context (10 minutes)
1. Tell me about your school (size, type, location)
2. How long have you been in this role?
3. What are your main responsibilities?
4. How many staff members do you manage?

### Current State & Pain Points (15 minutes)
5. **Walk me through a typical day. What takes up most of your time?**
   - *Listen for: Administrative burden, manual tasks*

6. **What tools/software do you currently use for school management?**
   - *Listen for: Spreadsheets, multiple tools, integration issues*

7. **What are your biggest frustrations with current systems?**
   - *Listen for: Data entry, reporting, communication gaps*

8. **How do you track student performance and attendance?**
   - *Listen for: Manual processes, delayed insights*

9. **How do you communicate with teachers, students, and parents?**
   - *Listen for: Fragmented channels, information loss*

10. **What reports do you need to generate? How long does it take?**
    - *Listen for: Time-consuming manual work*

### Desired State & Needs (10 minutes)
11. **If you could wave a magic wand, what would your ideal school management system do?**
    - *Listen for: Automation, integration, insights*

12. **What features are absolutely critical for you?**
    - *Listen for: Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves*

13. **How important is mobile access to you?**
    - *Listen for: On-the-go needs*

14. **Would you be interested in AI-powered features like automated grading or tutoring?**
    - *Listen for: Openness to innovation, concerns*

### Decision Making & Budget (5 minutes)
15. **Who is involved in deciding on new software for the school?**
    - *Listen for: Decision-makers, influencers*

16. **What's your budget range for school management software?**
    - *Listen for: Price sensitivity, payment preferences*

17. **What would make you switch from your current system?**
    - *Listen for: Key drivers, deal-breakers*

### Closing (5 minutes)
18. **Is there anything else you'd like to share about your challenges or needs?**

19. **Would you be interested in being a pilot school for our platform?**

20. **Can I follow up with you as we develop the product?**

## Teacher Interview Guide

### Introduction (3 minutes)
- Thank you for your time
- Purpose: Understanding teaching and grading challenges
- Confidentiality assurance

### Background (5 minutes)
1. What subjects and grades do you teach?
2. How many students do you have in total?
3. How long have you been teaching?

### Current Workflows (15 minutes)
4. **Walk me through how you prepare and deliver a lesson.**
   - *Listen for: Material creation, sharing methods*

5. **How do you create and grade assessments?**
   - *Listen for: Time spent, manual grading burden*

6. **How much time do you spend grading per week?**
   - *Listen for: Quantify pain point*

7. **How do you track student attendance?**
   - *Listen for: Manual processes, time waste*

8. **How do you communicate with students and parents?**
   - *Listen for: Channels used, effectiveness*

9. **How do you identify students who are struggling?**
   - *Listen for: Early intervention challenges*

### Pain Points (10 minutes)
10. **What's the most time-consuming part of your job (non-teaching)?**
    - *Listen for: Administrative burden*

11. **What frustrates you most about current tools or processes?**
    - *Listen for: Specific pain points*

12. **Do you feel you have enough time for actual teaching?**
    - *Listen for: Work-life balance*

### Desired Features (10 minutes)
13. **If you could automate one task, what would it be?**
    - *Listen for: Top priority*

14. **Would AI-assisted grading be helpful? Any concerns?**
    - *Listen for: Openness, trust issues*

15. **How important is it to have all teaching materials in one place?**
    - *Listen for: Organization needs*

16. **Would you use an AI tutor for students? Why or why not?**
    - *Listen for: Value perception*

### Closing (2 minutes)
17. **Anything else you'd like to share?**

18. **Can we follow up with you for feedback?**

## Student Interview Guide

### Introduction (2 minutes)
- Thank you for your time
- Purpose: Understanding how you learn and study
- No right or wrong answers

### Background (3 minutes)
1. What grade are you in?
2. What are your favorite subjects? Least favorite?
3. How do you usually study?

### Current Experience (10 minutes)
4. **How do you access your homework and assignments?**
   - *Listen for: Access methods, challenges*

5. **What do you do when you don't understand something?**
   - *Listen for: Help-seeking behavior*

6. **Do you ever feel shy asking questions in class?**
   - *Listen for: Barriers to learning*

7. **How do you keep track of assignment deadlines?**
   - *Listen for: Organization challenges*

8. **What apps or websites do you use for studying?**
   - *Listen for: Current tools, preferences*

### Desired Features (10 minutes)
9. **Would you use a chatbot that answers your questions anytime?**
   - *Listen for: Interest level, use cases*

10. **How do you prefer to learn - videos, text, or interactive?**
    - *Listen for: Learning preferences*

11. **Would you like to see your progress and grades online?**
    - *Listen for: Transparency needs*

12. **What would make studying more fun or easier for you?**
    - *Listen for: Engagement ideas*

### Closing (2 minutes)
13. **Anything else you'd like to tell us?**

   - *Listen for: Information gaps*

   - *Listen for: Payment pain points*

8. **What school information do you wish you had easier access to?**
   - *Listen for: Transparency needs*

### Desired Features (10 minutes)
9. **Would you use a mobile app to track your child's progress?**
   - *Listen for: Mobile preference*

10. **How often would you like to receive updates?**
    - *Listen for: Communication frequency*

11. **Would you pay fees online if available?**
    - *Listen for: Digital payment adoption*

12. **What would help you support your child's learning at home?**
    - *Listen for: Engagement opportunities*

### Closing (2 minutes)
13. **Anything else you'd like to share?**

## Interview Best Practices

### Before the Interview
- [ ] Research the school/person
- [ ] Prepare customized questions
- [ ] Test recording equipment
- [ ] Send calendar invite with agenda
- [ ] Confirm 24 hours before

### During the Interview
- [ ] Start with rapport building
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions
- [ ] Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
- [ ] Probe deeper with "Why?" and "Tell me more"
- [ ] Take notes on exact quotes
- [ ] Watch for non-verbal cues
- [ ] Stay neutral, don't pitch
- [ ] Respect time limits

### After the Interview
- [ ] Send thank you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Transcribe notes while fresh
- [ ] Identify key themes and quotes
- [ ] Update persona documents
- [ ] Share insights with team
- [ ] Follow up on any commitments

## Synthesis Framework

### After 5-10 Interviews
- Identify recurring themes
- Create affinity diagrams
- Update personas
- Refine problem statement

### After 20+ Interviews
- Quantify pain points (% of respondents)
- Prioritize features by demand
- Validate MVP scope
- Identify early adopters

### Key Questions to Answer
1. What are the top 5 pain points?
2. What features are must-haves?
3. What's the willingness to pay?
4. Who are the decision-makers?
5. What are the adoption barriers?

---

## Validation Criteria

### Problem Validation
- [ ] 80%+ of interviewees confirm the same pain points
- [ ] Current solutions are inadequate
- [ ] Pain is severe enough to pay for solution
- [ ] Problem occurs frequently (daily/weekly)

### Solution Validation
- [ ] 70%+ express interest in proposed features
- [ ] Willingness to switch from current tools
- [ ] Budget aligns with pricing model
- [ ] 5+ schools commit to pilot program

## Next Steps After Interviews

1. **Synthesize Findings** (Week 1)
   - Compile all notes
   - Create themes document
   - Update personas
   - Refine problem statement

2. **Validate with Prototypes** (Week 2-3)
   - Create wireframes
   - Conduct usability tests
   - Gather feedback
   - Iterate designs

3. **Finalize MVP Scope** (Week 4)
   - Prioritize features based on feedback
   - Create detailed requirements
   - Get stakeholder sign-off

4. **Recruit Pilot Schools** (Week 5)
   - Reach out to interested schools
   - Negotiate pilot terms
   - Set success criteria
   - Plan onboarding
